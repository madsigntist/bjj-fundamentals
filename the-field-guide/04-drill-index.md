## Level 1 - Foundations

### Drill 1: Full Sequence Flow (No Resistance)

**Purpose**: Learn the seven-step foundational sequence in order with a cooperative partner.

**Format**: Start in bottom side control or bottom mount. Walk through all seven steps slowly: Hierarchy → Base → Posture → Frames → Movement → Inside position → Head-hips. Five repetitions per position, then switch.

**Focus**: Correct order, not speed. The sequence must become automatic.

**Concepts**: All Level 1 concepts in integration order.

---

### Drill 2: Constraint Drill (Enforced Sequence)

**Purpose**: Build the discipline of following the correct order under light pressure.

**Format**: Partner applies light resistance from side control or mount. Student must follow the sequence in order: no movement before frames are built, no escape before posture is fixed, no guard recovery before inside position is established. If any step is skipped, the partner stops the round and the student resets.

**Focus**: Discipline of sequence under pressure.

**Concepts**: Sequence enforcement: frames before movement, posture before escape.

---

### Drill 3: Positional Awareness Drill

**Purpose**: Build the mental map of the positional hierarchy.

**Format**: Partner places the student in random positions (side control, mount, turtle, guard, half guard). Student immediately identifies (bad, neutral, or dominant, then states the first three priorities based on the hierarchy. No physical escape) recognition and planning only.

**Focus**: Mental speed in positional assessment.

**Concepts**: Positional hierarchy, threat recognition.

---

### Drill 4: Structure Retention Drill

**Purpose**: Develop the ability to maintain structural integrity under increasing pressure.

**Format**: Start in bottom side control. Partner applies increasing pressure (30%, 50%, 70%). Student's only goal (maintain base, posture, and frames for 60 seconds. No escape attempts) only structural maintenance. After the round, identify which structural element broke down first.

**Focus**: Endurance of structure, not escape.

**Concepts**: Base, posture, frames under load.

## Level 2 - Defense and Escape

### Drill 5: Full Sequence Flow (No Resistance)

**Purpose**: Learn the six-step escape sequence in order with a cooperative partner.

**Format**: Start in side control, mount, or back control. Walk through all six steps slowly: Survive → Align → Frame → Hand fight → Elbow-knee → Recover. Five repetitions per position, then switch.

**Focus**: Correct order, not speed.

**Concepts**: All Level 2 concepts in integration order.

---

### Drill 6: Constraint Drill (Enforced Sequence)

**Purpose**: Enforce the escape sequence under moderate resistance.

**Format**: Partner applies moderate resistance from side control or mount. Student must follow the sequence in order. No hip escape before frames are built. No guard recovery before elbow-knee connection. If a step is skipped, stop the round and reset.

**Focus**: Discipline of sequence under real pressure.

**Concepts**: Sequence enforcement: survive before escape, frame before move.

---

### Drill 7: Timed Escape Drill

**Purpose**: Develop speed in the full escape sequence under a time constraint.

**Format**: Start in a bad position. Complete the full six-step sequence within 60-90 seconds. Track which step takes the longest: that is where to focus practice. Repeat with increasing partner resistance each round.

**Focus**: Speed and efficiency in the escape process.

**Concepts**: Full escape integration under time pressure.

---

### Drill 8: Layer Reset Drill

**Purpose**: Diagnose which step in the escape sequence consistently fails.

**Format**: Partner escapes from bottom at moderate effort. After each escape (successful or not), stop and diagnose: which of the six steps broke down? Survival skipped? Alignment ignored? Frames collapsed? Grips not cleared? Elbow-knee missed? Guard recovery incomplete? Log the pattern.

**Focus**: Identifying the weakest link in the escape chain.

**Concepts**: Diagnostic: all Level 2 concepts.

---

### Drill 9: Escape-to-Guard Sparring (Positional)

**Purpose**: Full escape-to-guard recovery under real resistance.

**Format**: Start in a bad position. Student's goal (reach guard using the full sequence. Partner's goal) maintain the dominant position. The escape is only counted as successful if the student reaches a functional guard, not just creating space. Three-minute rounds. Track success rate.

**Focus**: Complete escape-to-recovery execution.

**Concepts**: All Level 2 concepts applied in live conditions.

---

## Level 3 - Positional Control

### Drill 10: The Complete Pin (5-Minute Rounds)

**Purpose**: Develop sustained control using all five Level 3 concepts simultaneously.

**Format**: Hold side control, mount, or back for a full 5-minute round. No submissions allowed: control only. Partner escalates effort each minute: 20%, 40%, 60%, 80%, 100%. After the round, identify which concept broke down first when pressure increased.

**Focus**: Complete control endurance.

**Concepts**: Pinning mechanics, head-hip control, weight distribution, connection, pre-empting escapes.

---

### Drill 11: Position Reset Flow

**Purpose**: Maintain control while transitioning between dominant positions.

**Format**: Start in side control (hold 30 seconds. Transition to mount without losing connection) hold 30 seconds. Transition to back control without losing connection (hold 30 seconds. Return to side control. Repeat for 5 minutes. Rule) never break body contact during transitions.

**Focus**: Fluid transition between dominant positions.

**Concepts**: Connection maintenance during transitions.

---

### Drill 12: Diagnostic Drill

**Purpose**: Identify which control concept consistently fails.

**Format**: Partner escapes from bottom at moderate effort. After each escape, stop and diagnose: which of the five concepts failed? Pin structure wrong? Head or hips free? Weight in the wrong place? Connection lost? Attacked too early? Log the pattern.

**Focus**: Diagnosing control breakdowns.

**Concepts**: Diagnostic: all Level 3 concepts.

---

## Level 4 - Guard Systems

### Drill 13: Full Guard Flow (6-Minute Rounds)

**Purpose**: Full guard integration under progressive resistance.

**Format**: Start in closed guard. Passer works to pass at progressive intensity (50% → 75% → 100% by the end). Guard player must use all six concepts: manage distance, keep legs inside, maintain connections, off-balance, retain layers when broken, and choose sweep or submission based on what the passer gives. After each round, identify which concept broke down first.

**Focus**: Complete guard operation under increasing pressure.

**Concepts**: All Level 4 concepts integrated.

---

### Drill 14: Guard Reset Drill

**Purpose**: Practice the full recovery-to-offense cycle from a nearly-passed position.

**Format**: Start at Layer 3 (guard nearly passed, only frames left). Guard player must climb all the way back to Layer 1 (feet on hips, grips established, inside legs). Once Layer 1 is re-established, attempt one off-balance → one sweep or submission attempt. Reset and repeat from Layer 3.

**Focus**: Recovery under pressure and offensive transition.

**Concepts**: Guard retention layers, recovery-to-offense cycle.

---

### Drill 15: Sweep-Submit Alternation Drill

**Purpose**: Build the habit of reading defenses and alternating between sweeps and submissions.

**Format**: From butterfly guard or closed guard, alternate between sweep attempt and submission attempt with each exchange. Exchange 1: attempt a sweep (partner defends). Exchange 2: use the defense reaction to attempt a submission (partner defends). Exchange 3: use the defense reaction to attempt a sweep. Continuous chain.

**Focus**: Reading defensive reactions and alternating threats.

**Concepts**: Sweep-vs-submission decision-making, reaction-based attacking.

---

### Drill 16: Connection Maintenance Under Pressure

**Purpose**: Build grip resilience and the reflex of replacing stripped connections.

**Format**: Guard player starts with four connections (two grips, two hooks). Passer's goal (strip all connections in 60 seconds. Guard player's goal) maintain at least two connections at all times. If connections drop below two, stop and reset.

**Focus**: Grip endurance and connection replacement speed.

**Concepts**: Connection points, guard retention.

---

## Level 5 - Passing Systems

### Drill 17: Full Passing Integration (6-Minute Rounds)

**Purpose**: Full guard passing using all five Level 5 concepts under live conditions.

**Format**: Start standing or kneeling in the guard player's open guard. Passer must use all five concepts: establish control, work through the gates, create separation, clear frames, and choose the right approach. Guard player works full retention. After each round, the passer identifies which concept broke down first when the pass failed.

**Focus**: Complete passing integration.

**Concepts**: All Level 5 concepts integrated.

---

### Drill 18: Gate Identification Drill

**Purpose**: Build conscious awareness of the gate sequence during live passing.

**Format**: Passer works to pass at 50% intensity. At any point, the coach calls "Freeze." Passer must identify: which gate am I at? What is blocking me? What do I do next? Reset and continue.

**Focus**: Cognitive awareness of passing phase.

**Concepts**: Gate sequence (feet → knees → hips → upper body).

---

### Drill 19: Pressure-to-Movement Switching Drill

**Purpose**: Develop the ability to change passing approaches mid-pass.

**Format**: Passer starts a pressure pass (knee cut, over-under, or bodylock). At any point, the coach calls "Switch." Passer immediately transitions to a movement pass (torreando, leg drag, or x-pass). On the next call, switch back to pressure.

**Focus**: Approach flexibility without losing control.

**Concepts**: Pressure vs movement passing, adaptability.

---

### Drill 20: Frame Clearing Under Pressure

**Purpose**: Isolate and practice the frame-clearing phase of passing.

**Format**: Start with the passer past the legs but not yet in side control. Guard player uses only upper body frames (no leg re-insertion). Passer must clear the frames and establish side control within 15 seconds. Guard player replaces frames as fast as possible.

**Focus**: Speed and urgency in frame clearing.

**Concepts**: Clearing frames before advancing.

---

### Drill 21: Re-Guard Prevention Drill

**Purpose**: Maintain position after completing a pass and prevent guard recovery.

**Format**: Passer completes a pass to side control. Guard player immediately tries to re-guard. Passer must maintain the position for 10 seconds and then re-pass if the guard is recovered.

**Focus**: Preventing re-guarding, pass consolidation.

**Concepts**: Control after passing, connection maintenance.

---

## Level 6 - Submission Systems

### Drill 22: Five-Phase Submission Drill

**Purpose**: Develop the explicit five-phase submission habit.

**Format**: From a dominant position (mount, side control, or back), execute a submission of choice at 30% intensity. Complete all five phases explicitly and in order. Call out each phase as you enter it: "Isolating... Exposing... Breaking defense... Maintaining control... Finishing." Partner provides moderate resistance and announces when each phase is successful.

**Focus**: Explicit five-phase sequence before it becomes automatic.

**Concepts**: All Level 6 concepts: isolation, exposure, breaking defense, control, mechanical efficiency.

---

### Drill 23: Submission Diagnostic Drill

**Purpose**: Develop analytical thinking about why submissions fail.

**Format**: Attacker attempts a submission and fails. Both partners freeze in position. Attacker diagnoses the failure using the Decision-Making Checklist ("Isolation was good. Exposure was incomplete) the arm is still bent. I need to extend before trying to finish." Reset from the same position and address the identified gap.

**Focus**: Post-failure analysis and correction.

**Concepts**: Diagnostic: all Level 6 concepts.

---

### Drill 24: Efficiency Comparison Drill

**Purpose**: Develop awareness of efficient vs inefficient mechanics by experiencing both.

**Format**: Execute the same submission twice. First (using only arms and grip (deliberately inefficient). Second) using hips and core (efficient). Note the difference in effort, force, and finishing speed.

**Focus**: Feeling the difference between muscle and mechanics.

**Concepts**: Mechanical efficiency.

---

### Drill 25: Strangle and Lock Circuit (5-Minute Rounds)

**Purpose**: Build fluency across submission types and the habit of transitioning between attacks.

**Format**: 5-minute rounds. Attacker cycles through four submissions: rear naked choke, armbar, triangle, kimura. For each one, complete the full five-phase sequence. If isolation fails, return to control position and try a different submission. If a defense holds for 15 seconds despite correct isolation and exposure, transition to the next submission.

**Focus**: Submission cycling and transition fluency.

**Concepts**: All Level 6 concepts across multiple submission types.

---

### Drill 26: Position-to-Submission Flow Drill

**Purpose**: Connect passing, control, and submission into one continuous sequence.

**Format**: Start from a passing position. Pass the guard (Level 5) → establish control (Level 3) → execute a submission (Level 6). The full chain: pass → control → isolate → expose → break → maintain → finish.

**Focus**: Cross-level integration (Levels 5 + 3 + 6).

**Concepts**: Passing-to-control-to-submission pipeline.

---

## Level 7 - System Integration

### Drill 27: Full System Rounds (8 Minutes)

**Purpose**: The capstone drill: a full match simulation using every concept from all seven levels.

**Format**: 8-minute rounds simulating a full match with all three phases (opening, middle, closing). After the round, both partners debrief using the Decision-Making Checklist: at each major moment, which concepts were used? Which were missed? Where did the system break down?

**Focus**: Complete system operation.

**Concepts**: All curriculum concepts integrated.

---

### Drill 28: Dilemma Chain Drill

**Purpose**: Build fluency in the offensive engine: dilemma creation, chaining, and anticipation combined.

**Format**: From any position, execute a chain of at least 3 dilemmas in sequence. Each dilemma must present two real threats. Each defense must flow into the next dilemma. The chain must be continuous: no resets, no holds, no pauses. Partner provides 60% resistance and makes the predicted defenses.

**Focus**: Continuous offensive pressure.

**Concepts**: Dilemma creation + chaining attacks + anticipating reactions (Concepts 1+2+3).

---

### Drill 29: Transition Exploitation Drill (5 Minutes)

**Purpose**: Force both players to see and act during transition moments rather than settled positions.

**Format**: 5-minute rounds where both partners are scored only on what happens during transitions, not during settled positions. Sweeps, passes, back takes, and submissions during transitions count. Anything from a settled position does not.

**Focus**: Recognizing and exploiting transition windows.

**Concepts**: Transition awareness.

---

### Drill 30: Pace Control Drill (6 Minutes)

**Purpose**: Build awareness of tempo as a strategic variable.

**Format**: 6-minute rounds where one player declares their pacing strategy ("I am going to control the pace by slowing this down" or "I am going to control the pace with burst-and-recover." After the round, both partners assess) who actually controlled the pace? At what moments did control shift?

**Focus**: Intentional tempo management.

**Concepts**: Strategic pacing.

---

### Drill 31: Energy Budget Drill (8 Minutes)

**Purpose**: Build awareness of energy as a finite, trackable resource.

**Format**: 8-minute round where the attacker self-rates their energy from 1-10 at 2-minute intervals (2, 4, 6, 8 minutes). Plot the energy curve after the round. Discuss: where was energy wasted? Where was it well spent? Did they have energy left at minute 8?

**Focus**: Energy management awareness.

**Concepts**: Energy efficiency.

---

### Drill 32: Complete Offensive System Drill (10 Minutes)

**Purpose**: Practice the entire integrated system as a single continuous flow.

**Format**: Start from a neutral position (both kneeling). Execute the full sequence: pass → control → recover → burst → dilemma → chain → submission. The entire sequence must use anticipation (pre-positioning), transition awareness (upgrading during transitions), energy efficiency (structure over muscle), and pacing (burst-and-recover). 10-minute rounds with progressive resistance (50% → 75% → 100%).

**Focus**: Complete system execution from start to finish.

**Concepts**: All Level 7 concepts applied in a single continuous flow.
