# Training Plan

---

A suggested schedule for working through the entire curriculum. This plan assumes the student trains 3 times per week. Adjust the timeline based on training frequency, partner availability, and individual learning speed. The key principle: do not advance until the current level feels natural under moderate resistance.

---

## Overview

| Phase   | Levels | Duration    | Focus                        |
| ------- | ------ | ----------- | ---------------------------- |
| Phase 1 | 1-2    | Weeks 1-10  | Survival, structure, escape  |
| Phase 2 | 3      | Weeks 11-18 | Positional control           |
| Phase 3 | 4      | Weeks 19-28 | Guard systems                |
| Phase 4 | 5      | Weeks 29-38 | Passing systems              |
| Phase 5 | 6      | Weeks 39-48 | Submission systems           |
| Phase 6 | 7      | Weeks 49+   | System integration (ongoing) |

**Total structured curriculum**: approximately 12 months at 3 sessions per week.

**Level 7 is ongoing**: system integration is a career-long practice. The structured work ends but the refinement does not.

---

## Phase 1: Foundations and Defense (Weeks 1-10)

### Weeks 1-2: The Blueprint + Level 1 Concepts 1-3

**Reading**: Introduction, How to Use This Book, Core Mechanical Concepts, Positional Hierarchy

**Concepts**: Positional hierarchy, base and posture, frames and distance

**Session structure** (each session):

- 15 min: Read and discuss one concept (partner or coach reviews together)
- 20 min: Drill — Structure Retention Drill (Drill 4) or Positional Awareness Drill (Drill 3)
- 25 min: Positional sparring from bad positions — survival only, no escapes yet

### Weeks 3-4: Level 1 Concepts 4-7

**Concepts**: Movement fundamentals, inside position, head and hip control, movement hierarchy (if applicable)

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Full Sequence Flow Drill (Drill 1) — all seven steps
- 25 min: Constraint Drill (Drill 2) — enforced sequence under light resistance

### Week 5: Level 1 Integration

**Reading**: Level 1 integration section

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Review the integration section together
- 25 min: All four Level 1 drills — rotate through
- 25 min: Positional sparring from bad positions — using the full Level 1 sequence

### Weeks 6-8: Level 2 Concepts

**Concepts**: Survival before escape, frame creation, alignment under pressure, elbow-knee connection, guard recovery, hand fighting

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review (one concept per session)
- 20 min: Full Sequence Flow (Drill 5) for escape
- 25 min: Escape-to-Guard Sparring (Drill 9) — start in bad positions, goal is to reach guard

### Weeks 9-10: Level 2 Integration + Phase 1 Review

**Reading**: Level 2 integration section, Universal Decision Framework

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Integration review
- 20 min: Level 2 drills — Timed Escape (Drill 7) and Layer Reset (Drill 8)
- 30 min: Full Phase 1 live rolling — start in bad positions, use all L1+L2 concepts

**Phase 1 checkpoint**: The student should be able to survive 3 minutes in any bad position without panicking, and escape to guard at least 50% of the time against a similarly-skilled partner.

---

## Phase 2: Positional Control (Weeks 11-18)

### Weeks 11-14: Level 3 Concepts

**Concepts**: Pinning mechanics, head-hip control together, weight distribution, preventing movement, connection and pressure

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: The Complete Pin (Drill 10) — 5-minute rounds, control only
- 25 min: Positional sparring from top positions — maintain control against escalating resistance

### Weeks 15-16: Level 3 Integration

**Reading**: Level 3 integration section

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Integration review
- 20 min: Position Reset Flow (Drill 11) — side control → mount → back → side control
- 30 min: Mixed positional sparring — start in both top and bottom positions, using L1-3 concepts

### Weeks 17-18: Phase 2 Review + Cross-Level Practice

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Review Decision Framework questions 1-3 (Am I safe? Inside position? Head-hip control?)
- 15 min: Diagnostic Drill (Drill 12) — identify control breakdowns
- 30 min: Open rolling with a focus on maintaining position — no submissions yet

**Phase 2 checkpoint**: The student should be able to hold side control against a resisting partner for 3+ minutes and transition between control positions without losing contact.

---

## Phase 3: Guard Systems (Weeks 19-28)

### Weeks 19-22: Level 4 Concepts 1-3

**Concepts**: Distance management, off-balancing, inside leg position

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Connection Maintenance Under Pressure (Drill 16)
- 25 min: Guard-specific positional sparring — closed guard, butterfly guard

### Weeks 23-26: Level 4 Concepts 4-6

**Concepts**: Connection points, retention layers, sweep-vs-submission decisions

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Guard Reset Drill (Drill 14) and Sweep-Submit Alternation (Drill 15)
- 25 min: Full Guard Flow (Drill 13) — 6-minute rounds

### Weeks 27-28: Level 4 Integration + Phase 3 Review

**Reading**: Level 4 integration section

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Integration review
- 20 min: All Level 4 drills — rotate through
- 30 min: Open rolling with a guard focus — start from guard, use all L4 concepts

**Phase 3 checkpoint**: The student should be able to maintain guard for 3+ minutes, execute at least one sweep and one submission attempt per round, and recover guard when partially passed.

---

## Phase 4: Passing Systems (Weeks 29-38)

### Weeks 29-32: Level 5 Concepts 1-3

**Concepts**: Control before passing, gate sequence (beat feet → knees → hips → upper body), separating knees from elbows

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Gate Identification Drill (Drill 18) and Frame Clearing (Drill 20)
- 25 min: Passing-specific positional sparring

### Weeks 33-36: Level 5 Concepts 4-5

**Concepts**: Clearing frames, pressure vs movement passing

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Pressure-to-Movement Switching (Drill 19)
- 25 min: Full Passing Integration (Drill 17) — 6-minute rounds

### Weeks 37-38: Level 5 Integration + Phase 4 Review

**Reading**: Level 5 integration section

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Integration review
- 20 min: Re-Guard Prevention (Drill 21) and Position-to-Submission Flow (Drill 26 preview)
- 30 min: Full passing rounds — start standing in guard, pass and establish control

**Phase 4 checkpoint**: The student should be able to pass the guard at least once per 5-minute round and maintain position after the pass. They should be able to identify which gate blocked them when a pass fails.

---

## Phase 5: Submission Systems (Weeks 39-48)

### Weeks 39-42: Level 6 Concepts 1-3

**Concepts**: Isolation before attack, exposure before finishing, breaking defensive structures

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Five-Phase Submission Drill (Drill 22) — call out each phase
- 25 min: Submission-specific positional sparring from dominant positions

### Weeks 43-46: Level 6 Concepts 4-5

**Concepts**: Control during submission, mechanical efficiency

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Efficiency Comparison (Drill 24) and Strangle and Lock Circuit (Drill 25)
- 25 min: Position-to-Submission Flow (Drill 26) — pass → control → submit

### Weeks 47-48: Level 6 Integration + Phase 5 Review

**Reading**: Level 6 integration section

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Integration review
- 20 min: Submission Diagnostic (Drill 23)
- 30 min: Full rounds — open rolling with a focus on finishing from dominant positions

**Phase 5 checkpoint**: The student should be able to identify all five phases during a submission attempt, diagnose what went wrong when a submission fails, and finish at least one submission per 5-minute round from a dominant position.

---

## Phase 6: System Integration (Weeks 49+)

### Weeks 49-52: Level 7 Concepts 1-3

**Concepts**: Dilemma creation, chaining attacks, anticipating reactions

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Dilemma Chain Drill (Drill 28) — 3+ dilemmas in sequence
- 25 min: Open rolling with offensive chain focus

### Weeks 53-56: Level 7 Concepts 4-6

**Concepts**: Transition awareness, energy efficiency, strategic pacing

**Session structure**:

- 15 min: Concept review
- 20 min: Transition Exploitation (Drill 29) and Pace Control (Drill 30)
- 25 min: Energy Budget Drill (Drill 31) — 8-minute rounds with self-rating

### Weeks 57+: Ongoing Integration

**Reading**: Level 7 integration section (re-read periodically)

**Session structure**:

- 10 min: Review the Decision-Making Checklist — pick two questions to focus on this session
- 20 min: Complete Offensive System Drill (Drill 32) — 10-minute rounds
- 30 min: Full System Rounds (Drill 27) — 8-minute rounds with post-round debrief

**No final checkpoint**: Level 7 is ongoing. The system deepens with experience. The drills and concepts are revisited continuously. The practitioner never "completes" Level 7 — they refine it for the rest of their training career.

---

## Weekly Session Template

For any week, a standard 60-minute session can follow this structure:

| Segment        | Duration | Content                                          |
| -------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Warm-up        | 5 min    | Movement — shrimps, bridges, stand-ups, rolls    |
| Concept review | 10 min   | Read/discuss the current concept                 |
| Drilling       | 20 min   | Level-specific drill from the Drill Index        |
| Positional     | 15 min   | Positional sparring focused on the current level |
| Open rolling   | 10 min   | Free sparring with concept focus                 |

Adjust the proportions as needed. During early levels, increase drilling time. During later levels, increase sparring time. The concept review is always present — even a 2-minute re-read before drilling improves focus.

---

## For Instructors: Class Structure

If teaching a group, the same weekly template works with one addition:

| Segment        | Duration | Content                                                                                                               |
| -------------- | -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Warm-up        | 5 min    | Group movement drill                                                                                                  |
| Concept lesson | 10 min   | Teach the current concept with demonstration                                                                          |
| Partner drill  | 20 min   | Level-specific drill — rotate partners                                                                                |
| Positional     | 15 min   | Positional rounds — winner stays, loser rotates                                                                       |
| Open rolling   | 10 min   | Free sparring — advanced students with beginners are encouraged to work on the current concept rather than dominating |

Run each level for the number of weeks suggested above before advancing the class. Students who join mid-cycle can be paired with advanced students who benefit from reviewing earlier levels.
