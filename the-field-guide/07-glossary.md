# Glossary

---

Definitions for terms used throughout the curriculum. Organized alphabetically.

---

**Americana** - A shoulder lock applied by isolating the opponent's arm against the mat and rotating the shoulder externally using a figure-four grip. Typically applied from mount or side control.

**Armbar** - A joint lock that hyperextends the elbow. The attacker controls the arm between their legs, hips against the elbow joint, and extends the arm by lifting the hips.

**Base** - The distribution of weight and contact points that creates stability. Wide base = hard to push over. Narrow base = easy to displace. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Body Triangle** - A control position from the back where the attacker's legs are crossed and locked around the opponent's torso in a figure-four configuration. Provides stronger hip control than standard hooks.

**Bow-and-Arrow Choke** - A collar choke applied from back control. One hand grips the collar behind the neck, the other controls the far leg, and the attacker extends to create the choking pressure.

**Bridge (Upa)** - A hip movement where the hips drive upward explosively from a supine position. Used to create space, destabilize the top player, or initiate escapes from mount and side control.

**Butterfly Guard** - An open guard position where the bottom player sits upright with both feet hooked inside the opponent's thighs. Used for sweeps (butterfly sweep, hook sweep) and entries into leg attacks or X-guard.

**Closed Guard** - A guard position where the bottom player's legs are wrapped around the opponent's waist with the ankles crossed. Provides strong control and access to sweeps and submissions.

**Collar Tie** - A grip on the back of the opponent's neck, typically behind the head. Used to control head position, break posture, and set up snaps and takedowns.

**Connection** - Physical contact between bodies. In the context of this curriculum: maximizing body-to-body contact to control (from top) or maintaining grip and hook contact to manage distance (from bottom). See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Crossface** - A control technique where the top player drives their shoulder and chest into the opponent's face/jaw, turning the head away. Prevents the bottom player from facing the top player and is a core component of side control.

**De La Riva Guard** - An open guard where the bottom player hooks one leg around the outside of the opponent's lead leg (foot behind the knee) while controlling the ankle. Named after Ricardo De La Riva.

**Dilemma** - A situation where two simultaneous threats are presented. The opponent can defend one but their defense exposes them to the other. See Level 7, Concept 1.

**Dog Fight** - A scramble position where both players are on their knees, each with an underhook on the same side. Common transition point from half guard. Can lead to back takes, sweeps, or re-guards.

**Elbow-Knee Connection** - The principle of connecting the elbow to the knee to create a defensive frame that prevents the opponent from passing or advancing. Central to guard recovery and escape sequences.

**Ezekiel Choke** - A strangle applied using the sleeve of the gi (or the blade of the hand in no-gi) by threading one arm behind the head and using the other to compress the neck. Can be applied from mount, inside guard, or top turtle.

**Frame** - A rigid structure created by the limbs to maintain or create distance. Effective frames use skeletal alignment (bone-on-bone) rather than muscular strength. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Grapevine** - A hook position from mount where the top player threads their feet inside the opponent's legs and hooks over the ankles, flattening the opponent and preventing bridging.

**Granby Roll** - A rolling inversion used to escape from turtle or bottom positions. The practitioner tucks and rolls over one shoulder to arrive facing the opponent in guard.

**Guard** - Any position where the bottom player uses their legs to control distance, prevent the pass, and create offensive opportunities. Includes closed guard, open guard variations (butterfly, De La Riva, spider, lasso, X-guard, etc.), and half guard.

**Guard Pass** - The act of defeating the bottom player's guard and arriving at a dominant position (side control, mount, or back). See Level 5.

**Guard Retention** - The ability to maintain the guard position when the opponent is attempting to pass. Uses frames, hip movement, leg pummeling, and connection maintenance. See Level 4, Concept 5.

**Guillotine** - A front headlock choke applied by wrapping the arm around the opponent's neck (typically when their head is down) and squeezing with the forearm against the throat or the side of the neck.

**Half Guard** - A guard position where the bottom player controls one of the opponent's legs between their own legs. Provides more control than no guard but less than closed guard. A common transition point between guard and side control.

**Headquarters** - A passing position where the passer kneels between the guard player's legs with one knee up and one knee down, maintaining balance and options to pass left or right.

**Heel Hook** - A leg lock that attacks the knee by controlling the foot and rotating the heel, placing torque on the knee ligaments. Illegal at all belt levels under IBJJF gi rules. Not covered in this curriculum.

**Hip Escape (Shrimp)** - A fundamental movement where the hips slide away from the opponent along the mat by pushing off one foot and driving the hips laterally. The primary movement for creating space from bottom positions.

**Hook** - A leg or foot position where the foot is inserted behind the opponent's knee, thigh, or hip to control movement. Back hooks control the bottom player's hips from back control. Butterfly hooks control distance from butterfly guard.

**Inside Position** - Having limbs positioned closer to the opponent's centerline than their limbs are to yours. The player with inside position controls the exchange. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Inversion** - The act of going upside down (shoulders on the mat, hips above the head) to recover guard, attack submissions, or escape passing attempts. Common in modern open guard systems.

**Kimura** - A shoulder lock applied by isolating the opponent's arm and rotating the shoulder internally using a figure-four grip (one hand on the wrist, the other reaching through). Can be applied from guard, side control, north-south, and other positions.

**Knee Cut (Knee Slice)** - A guard pass where the passer slides their knee across the opponent's thigh, pinning the bottom leg while driving the hips forward to arrive in side control.

**Knee Shield** - A half guard variation where the bottom player places their shin across the opponent's torso, creating distance and preventing the top player from flattening them. A key retention tool.

**Off-Balancing** - Disrupting the opponent's balance to create sweep and submission opportunities from guard. See Level 4, Concept 2.

**Lasso Guard** - An open guard where the bottom player wraps their leg over the opponent's arm and threads the foot behind the bicep. Creates strong control of one arm and limits the passer's mobility.

**Leg Drag** - A movement pass where the passer controls one of the opponent's legs and drags it across their body, clearing the legs and arriving in side control on the opposite side.

**Mount** - A dominant position where the top player sits on the opponent's torso. Provides weight advantage, gravity, and access to a wide range of submissions.

**North-South** - A top control position where the top player lies chest-to-chest on the opponent but oriented in the opposite direction (head to feet). Provides heavy control and access to chokes and arm attacks.

**Omoplata** - A shoulder lock applied from guard using the legs. The attacker's leg wraps over and around the opponent's shoulder, and the attacker sits up to apply pressure to the trapped arm.

**Open Guard** - Any guard position where the bottom player's legs are not locked around the opponent (as opposed to closed guard). Includes butterfly guard, De La Riva, spider, lasso, X-guard, and many others.

**Overhook (Whizzer)** - A grip where the arm wraps over the opponent's arm from the outside, hooking at the elbow or bicep. Used defensively against underhooks and offensively for throws, sweeps, and chokes.

**Pass** - See Guard Pass.

**Posture** - The alignment of the spine, head, and limbs that creates structural integrity. Good posture = straight spine, head over hips, structural connection. Broken posture = rounded spine, head forward, vulnerable to attacks. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Pressure** - The application of body weight and force against the opponent. Used from top positions to immobilize, exhaust, and create submission openings. Pressure passing uses weight rather than speed. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Rear Naked Choke (RNC)** - The highest-percentage submission in grappling. Applied from back control by threading one arm under the chin, connecting the hands behind the head, and squeezing the forearm against one carotid and the bicep against the other.

**Retention** - See Guard Retention.

**Scissor Sweep** - A sweep from closed guard where the guard player uses a knee shield and an opposite-side pull to tip the opponent over the bent knee.

**Seatbelt** - A grip configuration used in back control. One arm goes over the opponent's shoulder (overhook side), the other under the opposite armpit (underhook side), and the hands connect on the chest.

**Separation** - Creating distance between bodies. The opposite of connection. Used from bottom to create escape room and from top when movement passing requires space. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**Side Control** - A dominant position where the top player pins the opponent from the side with chest pressure, crossface, underhook, and hip control. The most common position reached after passing the guard.

**Single-Leg X (SLX)** - An open guard position where the bottom player controls one of the opponent's legs with both legs and an underhook or grip. Used for sweeps, entries to X-guard, and leg lock attacks.

**Spider Guard** - An open guard where the bottom player grips both of the opponent's sleeves and places their feet on the biceps, creating distance and controlling the arms.

**Sprawl** - A defensive movement where the hips are driven backward and the legs extend behind, flattening the body to prevent the opponent from completing a takedown or guard recovery.

**Submission** - A technique that forces the opponent to tap (concede defeat) or face injury/unconsciousness. Categories: strangles (blood/air chokes) and joint locks (arm, shoulder, leg).

**Sweep** - A technique executed from a bottom position (guard) that reverses the position, putting the bottom player on top. Sweeps typically exploit the opponent's off-balance using leverage and timing.

**Technical Stand-Up** - A method of standing from a seated or grounded position while maintaining a defensive structure (base hand posted, legs ready to frame). Used to safely return to standing from bottom positions.

**Torreando (Bullfighter Pass)** - A standing guard pass where the passer grips both of the opponent's legs below the knee and redirects them to one side while stepping around to the other side.

**Triangle** - A strangle applied by trapping the opponent's head and one arm between the legs, crossing the ankles, and squeezing the thighs together to compress the carotid arteries.

**Turtle** - A defensive position where the player is on hands and knees with limbs tucked, protecting the stomach and neck. A transitional position - recover guard or stand up quickly.

**Underhook** - A grip where the arm goes under the opponent's armpit from the inside. Underhooks provide inside position and control of the opponent's torso. The player with the underhook typically dictates the exchange.

**Wedge** - A body part inserted into a gap to block movement. Knee wedge against the hip prevents shrimping. Shin wedge across the waist prevents standing. See Core Mechanical Concepts.

**X-Guard** - An open guard position where the bottom player is underneath the opponent with both legs entangled in one of the opponent's legs, forming an X shape. Used for sweeps and transitions to leg attacks.
